# Troubleshooting and Diagnostics

Even the most perfect strategy can encounter technical or logical hurdles. If your bot is not behaving as expected, this guide will help you quickly identify and resolve the root cause.

---

## 1. The Bot is Running but Not Opening Trades

This is the most common question from users. Usually, the problem is not a system error, but overly strict rules within your strategy.

### Step 1: Check the Diagnostics Tab
Navigate to the **Diagnostics** section in the sidebar. Here, the system shows in real-time at which stage signals are being filtered out.
*   **Scenario A (Filters are blocking)**: You see that the signal generator is working, but the rejected signal count is growing. Check your global filters. Perhaps the `BTC State Filter` is active while the market is dropping, or the `Time Filter` prohibits trading during current hours.
*   **Scenario B (Foundations threshold too high)**: A signal is generated by indicators, but the system rejects it. Check the `Foundation Threshold` in the strategy settings. If the threshold is 80%, but your foundations (e.g., order book and tape) only score 50%, the trade will not open.
*   **Scenario C (No base signal)**: The strategy is built in a way that the conditions (AND/OR blocks) physically cannot coincide. Run it through the **Fast Vector Backtester** for the last month. If there are no trades there either, the strategy logic is flawed (e.g., RSI < 30 AND price is above the upper Bollinger Band).

## 2. Execution Errors (Trade Rejected by Exchange)

Sometimes the bot decides to enter a trade, but the exchange returns an error. These errors can be viewed in the **System Logs** section or in your notifications.

### Common Causes:
1.  **Insufficient Margin**: Your balance or the allocated risk per trade is too small.
    *   *Solution*: Check your Risk Management settings. If the risk is 1% and the deposit is $100, the bot will try to buy for $1, which is below the minimum order limit on Binance/Bybit. Increase your deposit or Leverage.
2.  **API Key Errors**: "Invalid API Key", "IP Restriction", or "Futures Trading Disabled".
    *   *Solution*: Go to Account -> API Keys. Ensure that the "Enable Futures" permission is checked for the key, and IP whitelisting is configured correctly (if used).
3.  **Reduce Only Order Rejected**: The bot is trying to close a position that doesn't exist. This happens if you manually closed the position via the exchange interface, and the bot didn't know about it.
    *   *Solution*: Use the "Sync Positions" button on the Live Trading tab.

## 3. Trade Closed Prematurely

Did you expect a 5% profit, but the position closed with a small loss or break-even?

### What to Check:
*   **Risk Management Limits**: Check global risk settings (Account -> Settings). It's possible the **Daily Stop Loss** (you exceeded your daily loss limit) or **Max Drawdown** was triggered. The platform's Risk Manager has the highest priority and will forcibly close all positions to protect your capital.
*   **Conditional Exits**: Check the Management section blocks in your strategy. Perhaps a `Time Exit` was activated, or a `Move to Break-Even` triggered during a micro-pullback.
*   **Regime Change**: If you are using the AI Oracle, it might have signaled an emergency market regime change (e.g., transitioning to a bearish phase), causing an automatic closure of all LONG positions.

---

### The Golden Rule
If you do not understand what is happening—**stop the bot** on the Live Trading tab and switch the strategy to **Paper Trading**. This allows you to safely debug the logic in real-time without risking actual capital.
